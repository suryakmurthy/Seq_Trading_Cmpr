Consent to Participate in Research

Study Title: Autonomous Negotiation using Comparison-Based Optimization

Conducted By: Surya Murthy, Ph.D. student, Department of Computer Engineering

Faculty Advisor: Ufuk Topcu, Professor, Department of Aerospace Engineering

You are invited to be part of a research study. This consent form will help you choose whether or not to participate in the study. It is totally up to you to decide to be in this research study. Participating in this study is voluntary. Your decision to participate will not affect your relationship with The University of Texas at Austin. You will not lose any benefits or rights you already had if you decide not to participate. Even if you decide to be a part of the study now, you may change your mind and stop at any time. You do not have to answer any questions you do not want to answer.

The purpose of the study:

We have developed an algorithm to facilitate negotiations between two parties. The two parties have a set of items that they trade with each other. The algorithm knows the preferences of one party and makes trade offers on behalf of the party. The algorithm aims to find satisfactory trades that would be beneficial for both parties. We would like to test our algorithm in settings with human negotiators to determine its strengths and limitations. In particular, we would like to test whether the algorithm can find mutually beneficial trades when negotiating with humans. We will measure information like the number of offers that the algorithm makes and the benefit it obtains with each successful trade and the human’s level of satisfaction with the trade.

If you agree to take part in this study, you will be asked to engage in a hypothetical trading scenario with an autonomous negotiation algorithm. There are no real-world implications associated with these scenarios. At the start of the trading scenario, you will be provided with a prompt that will describe the negotiation setting and motivate your trades with the algorithm. At the beginning of the scenario, you will start with a set of items that you will hypothetically own. To understand whether the trades are aligned with your preferences, you will also be asked for a target number of items you would like to obtain through trading.

During the negotiation process, our algorithm will provide trade offers, and you will respond using a text input within two minutes after each offer. If you accept the trade offer, you will exchange items with the other party and continue trading with the new item amounts. If you reject the offer, the algorithm will continue making offers. You can also provide the algorithm with additional feedback on the trade offers through text. The trading process will continue until you feel satisfied with the current number of items.

As a comparison to our algorithm, we use an existing large language model (e.g., ChatGPT) to facilitate negotiation. Instead of interacting with our algorithm, you may interact with the large language model. At the beginning of the scenario, you will be informed about who you are interacting with.

We expect that this study will take about 10-20 minutes. If you spend more than two minutes without providing a response to the algorithm, the study will end, and you will be redirected to the payment information screen.
